 Integrated Volunteering

Integrated Volunteering is a committee under TMS with a goal of raising awareness to medical causes through fundraising events during the years. Events such as Thanksgiving Pies and Valentine’s Candy Grams provide opportunities for students to be involved within the TAMS community, earn volunteer hours, and learn about various medical causes while donating to them. We can’t wait to work with you guys this year!

Denton Nursing Home

Denton Nursing Home is a collaboration between HOPE and TMS that facilitates interactions between TAMS students and residents at the Good Samaritan Society Denton Nursing Home. We will hold weekly volunteering opportunities where students may spend time with residents with games and other activities. DNH volunteering is a lot of fun and is definitely an opportunity that shouldn’t be missed.

TMS Shadows

Our committee strives to provide you with personalized shadowing opportunities that are geared towards your interests in the medical field. As a TMS Committee, we will connect you with doctors in the Denton/Dallas area  whom you can shadow throughout the school year. Outreach

TAMS Medical Society Outreach is a committee of the TAMS Medical Society. You’re supervising a playground filled with children when suddenly you hear a shriek. Sobbing in pain, a child lays before you, clutching his foot. The children around you are pale and bewildered looking only to you for help. What if. What if that didn’t happen. In fact, what if a responsible adult was already present, and all the kids were calm during this situation. The goal of TMS Outreach is to educate others about the importance of care and medicine through engaging lessons in health and first aid.

 Project Sunshine

Project Sunshine is a nonprofit TMS committee that helps children living with medical challenges by alleviating stress and fostering a sense of normalcy during their hospital stay. We do this by donating craft kits created by our student volunteers during volunteer GAs and holding fundraisers throughout the year to raise money. We hope that with our efforts we can make a difference in these children's and their families' lives.
Heads: Neha Kalakuntla and Maggie Wang.

FB Page: https://www.facebook.com/groups/139079506950246/

 

Cure International

Cure is a nonprofit organization that focuses on serving children with treatable medical conditions and disadvantages in underprivileged countries. Its chapter as a TMS Committee will fundraise and raise awareness for these children by partaking in the CURE Hero program, through which TAMS will be able to sponsor a child each month for $38. This committee will use various fundraising tactics to raise this money, giving the TAMS community the opportunity to directly change a suffering child's life. The 2018-2019 TMS CURE International. Children we’ve sponsored in the past include Innocent and Koubra. Read about them here: https://cure.org/curekids/malawi/2017/10/innocent/#getwell and https://cure.org/curekids/niger/2017/09/koubra/

Heads: Julius Jang and Joanna Huynh

FB Page: https://www.facebook.com/groups/2518249691733655/

 

Hearts for Arts

We organize art events at the Denton County Friends of the Family Shelter and Scottish Rite Hospital. We aim to brighten the atmosphere and help entertain families with volunteers helping at art stands, playing music or dancing on Fridays and Saturdays throughout the semester. CareCrafts works with children's hospitals in the the Denton and DFW area to bring joy to children in long-term hospital stay through small packaged crafts, such as origami cranes, cards or finger knitting. Once a month on Fridays, volunteers will go to Scottish Rite to make crafts with groups of kids. In addition, there will be volunteering opportunities throughout the year in MAC to make crafts that are delivered to the kids.

Heads: Koshma Eswaramoorthy, Madhu Narendran, Riya Kumar and Vi-Anh Hoang
