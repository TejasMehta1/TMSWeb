Medical Humanities Journal










The Story:

Historical and contemporary figures have stood at the intersection of medicine and the humanities to produce groundbreaking discoveries such as Leonardo da Vinci's Vitruvian Man, an icon of breathtaking poetic beauty and startling scientific accuracy. Literature, philosophy, history, drama, poetry, politics and rhetoric all contribute to the betterment of medicine. In an increasingly interdisciplinary modern world, doctor's job is not merely to be trained to perform medical procedures, but to be educated about the full depth and scope of the humanities.

Through this endeavor, TAMS Medical Society strives to ensure students think deeply, analyze critically and reflect often on their medical experiences (shadowing, interviews or even watching a medical TV show). An esteemed panel of judges across broad disciplines (english, education, medicine) will review written and artistic works critically. Select submissions will be featured in an annual publication and published authors will be invited to celebrate their success, present their creation and network with medical professionals at the magazine’s release party.

 

Here’s The Process:

  1. Started writing about your passions, thoughts, and curious wonderings about our world. Get ready to give others a taste of what goes on in you mind. Focus on our medical humanities theme as seen in “The Story.” Read example ideas here!

 

  1. Email your original submission to magazinesubmissions@tmsmedics.com along with your name, school and contact information. Expect a confirmation within one week and reach out if you have not heard back. Submission is free, but maximum number of entries per person is one. Once sent, you cannot edit a submissions.

DEADLINE: OCT 2nd 2018 11:59PM

 

  1. Your submission will be judged by an esteemed panel on the basis of creativity and adhering to the theme. Judges will not receive any personal information. Read about the judges, many of whom are TMS alumni, here!

 

  1. Find out if you have been selected for print publication or an award in December. You may choose to be published anonymously. 1st, 2nd and 3rd place will receive monetary compensation. Even if you are not selected, all serious entries will be featured on social media and can be entered into various medical writing competitions!

 

  1. Celebrate at the magazine launch party on January 18, 2018! You will pick up your copy of the publication and network with medical professionals. Those selected for publication will read short excerpts from their piece and plenty of refreshments will be provided.
